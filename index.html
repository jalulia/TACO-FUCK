OK SO WHAT AM I LOOKING AT

Two questions, one index:

FUCK: How bad is it right now?
TACO: Is it bad enough that he'll reverse course?

When both are high, you're at peak TACO FUCK: maximum chaos AND maximum pressure for a U-turn. Historically, that's when the reversal comes.

WAIT, TACO IS A REAL THING?

"Trump Always Chickens Out" was coined by Robert Armstrong of the Financial Times in May 2025. It describes a pattern: Trump announces something aggressive, markets tank, then he walks it back.

Traders started betting on this cycle — buying the dip, selling the bounce. It became the "TACO trade." Deutsche Bank built a formal Pressure Index (by Maximilian Uleer) to predict when the next TACO would come.

Trump was asked about it at a press conference. He called it a "nasty question."

AND THE FUCK PART?

F = Fear. The VIX. Normal is 12–18. It hit 52 during Liberation Day.

U = Unpredictability. How wildly bonds are swinging. When this is high, nobody knows what's coming next.

C = Consumer Collapse. Michigan Consumer Sentiment, flipped. When regular people feel terrible, C goes up.

K = Khaos. High-yield credit spreads. When lenders think companies might default. The K is intentional.

Each gets z-scored and averaged. One number: how fucked are things?

IS THIS REAL?

The data is 100% real — every number from the Federal Reserve's free database. The TACO concept is real (FT, Deutsche Bank). The FUCK backronym is ours. The weighting is arbitrary. It's not backtested.

Think of it as: the data is real, the framework is illustrative, the name is perfect.

ORIGIN STORY

Julia asked DeepSeek to build a "volatility-rhetoric coupling index." DeepSeek produced an official-looking formula called the VRCI — proper notation, working code. It was completely made up.

The TACO FUCK Index is the response: instead of a fake formula with fake data, a satirical framework with real data. Every number actually happened.
